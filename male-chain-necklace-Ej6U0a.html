    The Science Behind the Tint: How Do Transition Lenses Work for Prescription Glasses?
    At the core of how do transition lenses work for prescription glasses lies fascinating photochromic chemistry. These specialized prescription lenses contain billions of molecules—typically silver halide or organic photochromic compounds—that react to ultraviolet (UV) light. When exposed to UV rays, these molecules undergo a reversible chemical reaction, causing them to change shape and absorb visible light, thereby darkening the lens.
    The darker the UV light intensity, the darker your prescription photochromic lenses become. Conversely, when UV light is absent, such as indoors or at night, the molecules return to their original state, and the eyewear clears completely. This process is entirely automatic and continuous, providing optimal visual comfort and protection against harmful UV radiation at all times. This makes them a superior choice for many compared to carrying separate prescription glasses and sunglasses.
